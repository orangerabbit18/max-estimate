How the work breaks down

Three phases, plus companion video production. Discovery comes first on purpose — it's the phase that turns these ranges into firm numbers and makes the rest of the build predictable.

Phase 1
Discover & Clarify$5,394

Where the open questions become firm decisions. We lock the learning model, the production approach, the accessibility baseline, and the writing standards before a single lesson gets built — so the rest of the engagement runs clean.

  • Kickoff, project setup, and review of existing MAX modules and materials
  • Learning-model workshop with one or more prototype model options
  • Accessibility standard and inclusive-language writing guidelines
  • Focus-group data and learner personas folded into a living standards document
  • Video production approach decided
Phase 2
Prototype$4,301

Before scaling, we build one complete lesson end to end and test it with real law students. This proves the model and the per-lesson workflow, so the production phase moves with confidence rather than guesswork.

  • Production blueprint, script, and storyboard for the prototype lesson
  • Full build through the Alpha → Beta → Omega review cycle
  • Testing with representative law students
  • Iteration and near-final approval
  • Per-module coordination plan stood up

Excludes video production — the prototype lesson's video is counted in the companion video figure below.

Phase 3
Scale$68,211

The production engine, and the bulk of the engagement. Each remaining lesson moves through the same proven workflow, alongside the new downloadable action tools that send students off with something they can use right away.

  • 20 additional lessons built: SCORM reading and interaction design, in partnership with your subject-matter expertise
  • Infographic and visual design
  • Articulate build (Rise 360, with Storyline interactions where needed)
  • Copyedit, QA, and LMS development across the review cycle
  • New downloadable action tools (11 builds)

AI is part of how we design and build

AI isn't a bolt-on, it's woven through how we work. It lets a small, senior team move faster and spend our hours where judgment actually matters: original thinking, partnering with your subject-matter experts, and the craft of each lesson, rather than routine production and administration.

Across this engagement, that means using AI to:

  • Organize the project and keep it moving
  • Draft and sharpen writing, visuals, and imagery
  • Build and maintain the evidence-and-standards database that anchors every lesson
  • Pull the important data out of learner feedback, so decisions rest on data rather than hunches
  • Generally augment our, and your, teams as big and small challenges surface along the way

The result is work that's original and purpose-built for MAX — and a partner thinking alongside you, not just filling an order.
